# b2b-saas-ai-seo-playbook
# AI-Powered SEO Content Production Playbook (B2B SaaS)

## Project Overview
This repository serves as the foundational research for a modern B2B SaaS content playbook. The focus of this research is "AI-Powered SEO Content Production." 

However, this is not a guide on how to mass-generate cheap content. As search algorithms evolve into Answer Engines (AEO) and zero-click environments, modern SEO requires engineering content that Large Language Models (LLMs) can easily parse, trust, and cite.

## Repository Structure
The data is organized categorically to support the development of a comprehensive content pipeline:

* `/research/sources.md`: The curated master list of 10 industry practitioners.
* `/research/linkedin-posts/`: Scraped insights from top strategists focusing on programmatic SEO, E-E-A-T, and AI Visibility.
* `/research/youtube-transcripts/`: Extracted video transcripts detailing exact AI content workflows, prompt engineering, and community-led AI triggers (e.g., Reddit).
* `/research/other/`: Contains practical assets derived from the research, including a Master SEO Content Prompt that bridges text generation with advanced visual asset engineering.

## Why These Experts?
The primary evaluation criterion for this repository was to avoid generic "thought leaders" and instead curate a list of **genuine practitioners**. 

I specifically avoided voices who preach superficial "AI is the future" rhetoric or advocate for unchecked mass generation. Instead, the experts selected fall into three highly technical categories:

1. **The AI Content Strategists (e.g., Ross Simmonds, Kevin Indig):** Chosen because they provide data-backed case studies on how LLMs actually behave. They explain the shift from traditional keyword tracking to AI prompt tracking and the importance of off-site citations.
2. **The Hardcore Prompt Engineers & UGC Experts (e.g., Gael Breton, Matt Diggity):** Chosen for their actionable, step-by-step workflows. They share exact methodologies for bypassing AI detectors, maintaining tone of voice through progressive disclosure, and using platforms like Reddit as "AI Food" to manipulate LLM citations.
3. **Technical & Programmatic SEOs (e.g., Aleyda Solís):** Chosen for their ability to scale content quality through structured prompt libraries and API integrations without sacrificing brand authority.

The material collected here is highly tactical and provides more than enough robust data to build a real, executable B2B SaaS playbook.

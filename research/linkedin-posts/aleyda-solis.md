## Post 1: Building a Representative AI Search Prompt Library
* **Date:** June 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_how-to-build-a-representative-ai-search-activity-7469723521947131905-6DVK?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> One of the biggest mistakes I see with AI search tracking is using a non-representative prompt library to assess a brand’s AI search visibility. This can give you an inaccurate view of your AI search presence. 
> 
> A representative AI search prompt library should not be a random list of prompts or a tool’s default prompt set. It should be a structured sample of the AI-assisted journeys that matter to your business, covering the right products, audiences, markets, journey stages, competitors and buyer constraints. 
> 
> The goal is to measure the right prompts well enough to identify patterns, diagnose gaps and guide optimization actions.
> 
> A useful and often underused input for your AI Search prompt library: question-based queries that trigger Google AI Overviews, identified through keyword research tools with SERP feature filters (e.g., Semrush Keyword Magic Tool).

### Key Takeaway
To accurately measure and optimize a B2B SaaS brand's AI Visibility, marketers must move away from generic keyword lists. Instead, they need to engineer a highly structured, representative prompt library that mirrors actual, complex user intent—specifically leveraging question-based queries that trigger Google AI Overviews to diagnose content gaps.

## Post 2: Three Principles to Optimize for AI Visibility and Agentic Search
* **Date:** June 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_how-to-optimize-for-ai-visibility-and-activity-7468269284620734464-JpdJ?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> How to Optimize for AI Visibility and Prepare for Agentic Search 👇 I share Three principles to prioritize AI search optimization: 
> 
> 1. Stop using traffic as the main KPI for AI search impact.
> 2. Build topical authority with content that AI systems can easily retrieve, understand, and cite.
> 3. Strengthen Brand Authority through third-party corroboration.
> 
> In 2026, Less than One Third of Google Searches Still Send a Click (Excellent research by Rand Fishkin and Similarweb). Free yourself from the goal of directly driving traffic back to your website. Replace traffic as a KPI and invest in marketing on platforms you don’t own or control.

### Key Takeaway
As the web shifts toward zero-click searches and agentic retrieval, B2B SaaS SEO must pivot from chasing website traffic to engineering content for LLM ingestion. Success requires building absolute topical authority with structured assets that machines can parse easily, alongside aggressive off-site brand corroboration so LLMs trust and cite the brand.

## Post 1: AEO vs. SEO – Why AI Search is a Different Game
* **Date:** May 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_ad-hubspotmediapartner-activity-7465031776005144576-wa2z?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> Saying AEO and SEO are the same misses the point. Search rank still drives a lot of AI visibility, and most of the tactics overlap. But AEO is a different game.
> 
> Same between SEO and AEO: Technical accessibility, clear information architecture, content quality, authority signals, and user intent matching.
> 
> New in AEO:
> 1. Rank matters after inclusion: in shortlists, first still wins. In narrative answers, mention quality matters.
> 2. Mentions can rival links: reviews, comparisons, forums, articles, and docs can shape the answer.
> 3. Prompts are harder to track: they’re roughly 5x longer than keywords and harder to target page by page.
> 4. Positioning becomes machine-interpreted: AI infers your category, competitors, use cases, and buyer fit from the broader web.
> 5. Reputation gets compressed: owned content, third-party coverage, reviews, and consensus collapse into one answer.

### Key Takeaway
While traditional SEO fundamentals (technical health, architecture, quality) still apply, Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) fundamentally shifts the playing field. Brand mentions now rival backlinks, user prompts are vastly more complex than traditional keywords, and an AI's perception of a brand is synthesized from third-party consensus rather than just owned content.

## Post 2: How AI Reasoning Levels Change Buyer Journeys and TOFU Content
* **Date:** May 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_the-citation-gap-between-minimal-and-high-activity-7463215778679615489-vr0u?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> The citation gap between minimal and high reasoning isn't constant. It scales with funnel stage.
> 
> I ran 5 buyer journey stages (Problem, Exploration, Comparison, Validation, Selection) through GPT-5.2 twice, once with minimal reasoning, once with high.
> 
> 3 patterns held across 20 journeys:
> 1. Citation rate gap is +35pp at the Problem stage and only +5pp at Validation. High reasoning treats early-funnel prompts as research tasks. Minimal mode answers from memory.
> 2. Fan-out queries peak at Comparison: 24 sub-queries per response under high reasoning vs 5.5 under minimal.
> 3. Selection has the widest per-response variance.
> 
> A fan-out under high reasoning for a CRM comparison breaks into separate retrievals for API rate limits per vendor, SOC 2 compliance, SSO support, webhook architecture, OAuth... Each becomes its own search. The brand that wins isn't the one ranking for the parent prompt. It's the one whose docs surface clean for each sub-query.

### Key Takeaway
When LLMs use "High Reasoning" modes (such as in GPT-5.2), they execute dozens of granular "fan-out" micro-searches for specific technical criteria (e.g., API limits, SOC 2 compliance) rather than just looking at a top-level parent query. This revives the importance of Top-of-Funnel (TOFU) content and requires B2B SaaS brands to maintain highly detailed, easily retrievable documentation to win these complex AI evaluations.

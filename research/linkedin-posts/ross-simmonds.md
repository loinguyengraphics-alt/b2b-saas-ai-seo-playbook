# LinkedIn Posts: Ross Simmonds

## Post 1: Optimizing for the "AI Internet" vs. the Human Internet
* **Date:** June 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-aivisibility-chatgpt-activity-7466975551531094016-LBz8?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> Most marketers are still optimizing for the wrong internet. 
> 
> The one humans see is the surface: Pretty websites, sharp copy, tight design, storytelling that moves people. We've spent 20 years getting good at this layer. Someone at Cannes is going to get an award for this layer in a few weeks. 
> 
> The one AI reads is underneath.... Five levels deep. And it's where buying decisions are quietly being made right now.
> 
> When someone asks ChatGPT "who's the best [your category]?" Your homepage doesn't show up or even get cited. Your $500k creative campaign doesn't impact them. Your $40k white paper with quotes from execs isn't there. 
> 
> Build once for humans. 
> Build deeper for machines.
> 
> The question is whether you're building for both internets... Or still designing for one.

### Key Takeaway
Beautiful websites and expensive creative campaigns are essentially invisible to LLMs like ChatGPT. To win in AI search, B2B SaaS brands must shift their focus to building deep, highly structured content architectures ("build deeper for machines") that AI systems can easily parse, understand, and cite when answering buyer queries.

## Post 2: E-E-A-T Signals and the Power of Off-Site Citations
* **Date:** May 2026
* **URL:** [[LinkedIn post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-aivisibility-contentmarketing-activity-7465397670220713984--Flg?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADhDsxwBWtd0_Yz5DOzgsUvHTmTlXGb2_kM)]

### Content
> The most important factor for AI Visibility in 2026 👉🏿 🚨 EEAT SIGNALS 🚨
> 
> Hear me out: When a user submits a question to an LLM... The LLM modifies that question (query fan out) with a wide range of different queries, words & phrases. It looks for evidence that the information it's citing is valid and worth trusting. 
> 
> Brands who have spent the last few years ignoring the desire to publish 20,000 blog posts in a single month... and instead focused on building the most expert driven, research backed, high quality content possible are being cited by the LLMs at a higher rate than ever before. 
> 
> Brands who have done this on platforms OTHER than their own site are winning by an even BIGGER margin. In a recent study that we did with AirOps across B2B SaaS; we found that more than 80% of a categories high priority keywords in LLMs resulted in citations OUTSIDE of their own domain.
> 
> Remember: Different LLMs prefer different sites. We did one study and found that Perplexity loves LinkedIn. We did another study and found that Gemini loves YouTube. And we did another that found that ChatGPT loves Reddit.

### Key Takeaway
Mass-producing low-quality AI content is a flawed strategy. AI search engines heavily prioritize E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness) signals. More importantly, over 80% of LLM citations for B2B SaaS come from off-site sources. Brands must distribute expert content across third-party platforms (Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn) because LLMs rely on these diverse surfaces to validate brand authority.
